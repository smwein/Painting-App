# CoatCalc Demo — Voiceover Script

**Total duration:** ~80 seconds
**Tone:** Straight-talking tradesman. Short sentences. No fluff.
**Pacing note:** Leave a beat between sections. Read at a natural, confident pace — not rushed.

---

## Scene 1: Hook (0:00 – 0:05)
> CoatCalc. Bid. Paint. Profit.

## Scene 2: Problem (0:05 – 0:15)
> You're walking jobs, eyeballing numbers, scratching bids on the back of an envelope. Some you win. Some you leave money on the table. There's a better way.

## Scene 3: Measure (0:15 – 0:30)
> Walk the job. Punch in the square footage. Pick your options. That's it.

## Scene 4: Numbers (0:30 – 0:45)
> Labor, materials, and profit — calculated instantly. Your rates. Your margins. No guessing.

## Scene 5: Close the Deal (0:45 – 0:55)
> Export a clean PDF and hand it to the customer before you leave the driveway.

## Scene 6: Features (0:55 – 1:10)
> Five calculators for any job. Interior, exterior, per-room. Set your own rates and modifiers. Generate PDFs on the spot. Invite your whole crew — everyone shares the same pricing.

## Scene 7: CTA (1:10 – 1:20)
> Starting at twenty-nine bucks a month. Your whole team. Start free — no credit card. CoatCalc dot com.

---

## Recording Tips
- Record in a quiet room, close to the mic
- Keep it conversational — like you're talking to a buddy at a job site
- Don't "announce" — just talk
- Slight pauses between scenes give the editor room to breathe
- Total word count: ~130 words → comfortable at natural pace for 80 seconds

## Music Suggestions
- Upbeat but not cheesy — think confident, steady, modern
- Low energy during Problem scene, builds during Measure/Numbers
- Peak energy at Features, resolves at CTA
- Instrumental only — no lyrics competing with voice
- Search: "corporate upbeat background music" or "modern business promo track"
